Impressionism

Impressionism was a French artistic trend with emphasis on painting emphasis in painting that occurred at the time of the so-called "Belle Époque" (1871-1914). This trend played a very important role in the renewal of 20th century art. of 20th century art, being the great propeller of the so-called European avant-garde. The term "Impressionism" comes from a criticism of a work by Claude Monet, "Impression, Sunrise", from 1872.

Characteristics

The painters of Impressionist art used to produce their canvases in the open air. The intention was to capture the shades that objects reflect according to the the sunlight at certain times of the day. This movement was a watershed for painting. Its artists were not bound to the teachings of academic realism. However, they were influenced by the positivist currents of the second half of the of the second half of the 19th century, which excelled in precision and realism. This new artistic style competed with academic productions. To this end, There were places outside the traditional art circuits, such as the Salons, where impressionist painters held exhibitions showing their paintings. It is worth mentioning that the impressionist aesthetic guidelines are present in graphic graphic productions, in advertising, and other forms of mass communication. Up to the present day they continue to influence new aesthetics.

  • blurred outlines;
  • bright, colorful shadows;
  • strong contrasts between color and light determined by the law of complementary colors;
  • loose brush strokes;
  • mixtures of the paints made directly on the board;
  • the representation of everyday themes;
  • compositions inspired by photography.

Major Works and its Artists

Impression, Sunrise (1872–1872) - Claude Monet

Claude Monet was a French Impressionist painter, considered one of the most important artists of Impressionism. The term "Impressionism" came about because of his 1872 work "Impression, Sunrise". This is because at the First Impressionist Exhibition held in 1874 in Paris, the group of artists was pejoratively called "impressionists" by the critics. Among his main works are:

  • Women in the Garden (1866)
  • Impression, Sunrise (1872)
  • The Saint-Lazare Station (1877)

Luncheon of the Boating Party (1880-81) - August Renoir

Renoir was a very important French artist of the late 19th century. He had great prominence in painting and, along with other artists, continued a new way of painting, which became known as impressionism.
His works include:

  • The Parisian (1874)
  • Le Moulin de la Galette (1876)
  • Luncheon of the Boating Party (1880-81)
