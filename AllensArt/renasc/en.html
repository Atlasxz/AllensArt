Renaissance

The Artistic Renaissance represented one of the branches of the of the Renaissance period with the profusion of several works. Remember that the Renaissance was an artistic intellectual and cultural movement that began in the 15th century in Italy. It was with the decline of the feudal system and of several characteristics associated with the medieval period, that the Renaissance emerged, a period of cultural, artistic and scientific effervescence that spread that spread throughout Europe.

Characteristics

Unlike medieval art, the artistic renaissance was inspired by classical antiquity, that is, the Greco-Roman arts, which had been forgotten for centuries. For Renaissance artists, the context associated with the medieval period made it impossible for art to evolve in many ways. This is because the medieval period was closely related to a religious culture, where theocentrism (God in the center of the universe) ruled people's lives. It was from the scientific, social, and cultural advance that the Renaissance movement arose, which was marked above all by its humanistic character. Thus, medieval theocentrism gave way to Renaissance anthropocentrism, with the arrival of the Modern Age.

The great contribution of Renaissance art was the discovery of perspective and depth. Thus, from the straight and two-dimensional plane of medieval art, Renaissance art promoted a different look. Other no less important aspects explored by Renaissance artists were the balance of forms and the search for harmony, based on classical art. It is worth remembering that the artistic renaissance included the evolution of painting, sculpture, architecture, and literature, valuing human and natural aspects. Although many themes explored by Renaissance artists were associated with the religious and spiritual realm, the change in mentality of the time made it possible to include a variety of themes. Subjects ranged from customs, mythology, and landscapes, among others.
In a Nutshell:

  • Quest for perfection;
  • Anthropocentrism;
  • Realism;
  • Emergence of advanced techniques;
  • Rationality.

Major Works and its Artists

The Last Supper (1495-1498) - Leonardo da Vinci

Without a doubt, Leonardo was one of the main artists of the Renaissance. Renaissance painter, sculptor, architect, and literate, his main works include:

  • Mona Lisa (La Gioconda);
  • The Last Supper;
  • Vitruvian Man.
  • The Virgin of the Rocks;

Pietà (1498-1499) - Michelangelo

Michelangelo was a Renaissance painter, sculptor, architect and writer.
Considered the "Giant of the Renaissance", he stood out with the production of his sculpture of several works, among them, stand out:

  • David;
  • Pietà;
  • Moses;
  • Ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, highlighting the painting The Creation of Adam.

David (1430-1440) - Donatello di Niccoló

Donatello was an Italian sculptor born in Florence, was a prominent figure in Renaissance art.
Of his works, sculptures stand out:

  • David;
  • Gattamelata;
  • St. Mark's;
  • St. George's Tabernacle.

Madonna del Prato (1506) - Rafael Sanzio

Italian painter who, in his works, used the technique of contrasting light and shadows, being recognized as one of the main names of the Renaissance movement.
Among his works, it is worth mentioning the several paintings of Madonnas and The Marriage of the Virgin.
